# VII. Power Gaming

Power gaming is performing acts that are not human-like, forcing roleplay on others without a fair chance, making up things that did not happen to benefit yourself, or roleplaying things that are not possible script-wise to gain an unfair advantage.

## Examples

* Wearing a female ped while not roleplaying as a female.
* Hopping inside a vehicle to avoid being stabbed.
* Selling drugs on top of or below bridges and highways, on roofs or houses, on mountains, or in Green Zones.
* Moving or hiding dead bodies.
* Shooting from rooftops, hills, and mountains, which counts as high ground.
* Running into a house to avoid roleplay.
* Driving or running into water to avoid PD.
* Camping at a garage to wait for someone to pull out or stash a car and then rob them.

{% hint style="info" %}
Garage exception: if a car is left out at a garage and the owner is not seen near it, you may steal it.
{% endhint %}

## More Examples

* Running into a Green Zone after a shootout with police or a rival gang.
* Running into a Green Zone to avoid being killed.
* Shooting out of a Green Zone.
* Shooting off a roof or other high places.
* Dumping your car in water to save loot, such as during a PD chase after a robbery.
* Finding the quickest way to make money by exploiting instead of roleplaying around the city.

{% hint style="danger" %}
Power gaming removes the other player's fair chance to roleplay the situation. If your action only works because of a script limitation or an unrealistic advantage, do not do it.
{% endhint %}
