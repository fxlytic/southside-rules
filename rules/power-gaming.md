# VII. Power Gaming

**Power gaming** is performing acts that are **not human-like**, forcing roleplay on others without a fair chance, making up things that didn't happen to benefit yourself, or roleplaying things that aren't possible script-wise to gain an unfair advantage.

## Examples (Not Limited To)

* Wearing a **female ped** if you are **not roleplaying as a female**.
* **Hopping inside a vehicle to avoid being stabbed.**
* Selling drugs **on top of or below bridges and highways**, on **roofs/houses**, on **mountains**, or in **Green Zones**.
* **Moving or hiding dead bodies.**
* Shooting from **rooftops, hills, and mountains** (high ground).
* **Running into a house to avoid RP.**
* **Driving or running into water to avoid PD.**
* **Camping at a garage** to wait for someone to pull out / stash a car and rob them.

{% hint style="info" %}
**Garage exception:** If a car is left out at a garage and the **owner is not seen near the car**, you **may steal it**.
{% endhint %}

## More Examples

* Running into a **Green Zone** after a shootout with police or a rival gang.
* Running into a **Green Zone to avoid being killed**.
* **Shooting out of a Green Zone.**
* **Shooting off a roof** or high places.
* **Dumping your car in water to save loot** (e.g., during a PD chase after a robbery).
* **Finding the quickest way to make money (exploiting)** rather than roleplaying around the city.

{% hint style="danger" %}
Power gaming removes the other player's fair chance to roleplay the situation. If your action only makes sense because of a **script limitation or an unrealistic advantage**, don't do it.
{% endhint %}
